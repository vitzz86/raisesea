# RaiseSEA voice guide

**Voice principle:** the sharp friend who's been through it.

Direct, no fluff. Smart without being condescending. SEA-aware. Encouraging but honest. The voice should make founders feel sharper and more prepared — not coddled, not bureaucratic.

---

## The four voice principles

### 1. Direct, no fluff
Cut the corporate hedging. State what is, what to do, and what's next.

| ✗ Don't | ✓ Do |
|---|---|
| "We've successfully completed the analysis of your pitch deck." | "Analysis done. Score 67/100." |
| "It appears your financials section may benefit from additional detail." | "Your financials section needs work." |
| "Please feel free to upload your deck whenever you're ready." | "Upload your deck. Takes 2 minutes." |

### 2. Smart, not condescending
Assume the reader is a founder, not a beginner. Don't over-explain the obvious.

| ✗ Don't | ✓ Do |
|---|---|
| "A pitch deck is a presentation document used by entrepreneurs..." | (delete — they know what a pitch deck is) |
| "Click the button below to begin your fundraising journey." | "Start with a deck analysis." |
| "Don't worry — we're here to help!" | (delete — patronizing) |

### 3. SEA-aware
The product is built for Southeast Asian founders. The voice should reflect that — not by mentioning SEA constantly, but by avoiding US-centric defaults.

| ✗ Don't | ✓ Do |
|---|---|
| "Most US VCs prefer..." | "At your stage, investors typically look for..." |
| "Series A in the US averages $X million..." | "For SEA Series A, $X million is typical." |
| Mentioning Y Combinator as default reference | Mention Iterative, Antler, Wavemaker, NEN where relevant |

### 4. Encouraging but honest
Tell founders what's working AND what's not. Don't soften the hard truths — but pair them with a path forward.

| ✗ Don't | ✓ Do |
|---|---|
| "Great job! Everything looks amazing!" | "Solid run. Opening landed. Your closing slide needs work." |
| "Your deck has a few minor improvements possible." | "Three things to fix before you send this to investors." |
| "Unfortunately your score is low." | "Score is 42 — let's talk about why." |

## Voice by context

### Empty states
Format: **[State the situation] [Explain what's possible] [Single clear CTA] [Time estimate]**

✓ *"No analyses yet. Upload your deck — we'll show you how investors will see it, usually in under 2 minutes."*

✓ *"No investors tracked yet. Add your first one — we'll surface the ones that match your stage and sector."*

✗ *"You don't have any analyses yet. Please upload a pitch deck to get started!"* (too soft, generic)

### Error messages
Format: **[What happened] [Why, if known] [What to do]**

✓ *"That didn't go through. Network hiccup likely. Refresh in a few seconds."*

✓ *"Your deck couldn't be parsed. The PDF might be corrupted — try re-exporting from your slides tool."*

✗ *"An error occurred. Please try again."* (useless)

### Completion states
Format: **[Acknowledgment] [Key insight] [Path forward]**

✓ *"Good run. Your traction story landed — opening could be tighter. Here's what to work on next."*

✓ *"Analysis done. Score 67/100. Three concrete fixes will close the gap to 80 — they're below."*

✗ *"Analysis complete!"* (no information)

### Loading states
Format: **[What's happening] [Time expectation, if known]**

✓ *"Analyzing your deck — usually 30-60 seconds."*

✓ *"Generating your debrief — the AI is going slide by slide."*

✗ *"Loading..."* (lazy)

### CTAs (button labels)
Action verbs. Specific. Outcome-focused when possible.

| ✗ Don't | ✓ Do |
|---|---|
| "Submit" | "Upload deck" / "Save changes" / "Send request" |
| "Continue" | "Continue to analysis" / "Continue to debrief" |
| "Learn More" | "See how others structure this" / "Read the breakdown" |
| "Click Here" | (never use) |

## Spectrum: formality by context

```
FORMAL                                              CASUAL
   |                                                   |
[Legal/privacy]  [Settings]  [Main app UI]  [Empty states]  [Loading]
```

- **Legal/privacy/terms:** formal. Standard legal English.
- **Settings / billing:** semi-formal. Clear, neutral.
- **Main app UI (most copy):** the sharp-friend voice.
- **Empty states + loading:** can be slightly warmer / playful, but never silly.

## Words and phrases — always vs never

### Always
- "you" (talking to the founder directly)
- "your deck" / "your raise" / "your story" (possessive — they own this)
- specific verbs ("upload," "review," "practice," "track")
- specific outcomes ("score 67/100," "in under 2 minutes," "before your next meeting")

### Never
- "users" or "customers" (when talking about the founder reading the screen — they're not "a user", they're you)
- "leverage," "synergy," "robust," "best-in-class," "seamless" (corporate filler)
- "simply" or "just" before a verb ("just click here") — condescending
- "delight," "amazing," "awesome" (saccharine)
- "Oops!" or "Whoops!" as error openers — saccharine
- emoji as the entire icon for something (use Lucide icons; emoji only for badges/decoration)

---

## SEA-specific phrasing

### Always
- USD by default with local currency when context-relevant (IDR, SGD, MYR, VND, PHP, THB)
- "Southeast Asia" — full name, not abbreviated SEA when in marketing/landing
- Stage labels match SEA convention: "Pre-seed / Seed / Pre-Series A / Series A / Series B"

### Be aware
- "SEA" is fine in-app once context is established
- Don't assume English fluency — keep sentences short, avoid idioms ("ducks in a row," "ballpark figure")
- Don't anchor benchmarks to US figures without noting the geography

## Maintaining the voice

When new copy is written for a new screen:
1. Read it out loud. Does it sound like a person, or a corporate template?
2. Cut every word that doesn't change the meaning.
3. Check the "always / never" list above.
4. If in doubt, ask: "would I say this to a founder over coffee?" If no, rewrite.
